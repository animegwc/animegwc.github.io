Berserk

Set in a medieval Europe-inspired dark fantasy world, the story centers on the characters of Guts, a lone mercenary, and Griffith, the leader of a mercenary band called the "Band of the Hawk".Themes of isolation, camaraderie, and the question of whether humanity is fundamentally good or evil pervade the story, as it explores both the best and worst of human nature. Both the manga and anime adaptations are known for their use of graphic violence and sexual content.

    Season & Episode Episode Name Description
    S1:E1 " The Dragon Slayer" A few years after the events of a solar eclipse, one that foretold the coming of an angel of darkness, a lone swordsman walks into a bar where an elf is being tormented by a group of bandits. When the bandit leader threatens to kill a red-haired youth requesting membership, Guts beats the gang and takes his leave with the freed elf, named Puck, following him.
    S1:E2 "The Holy Iron Chain Knights" Despite his fatigue, Guts easily kills some of the Holy Iron Chain Knights, who have been sent by the Church of the Holy See to capture him. When Guts finds himself overwhelmed by their vice-commander Azan, he attempts a dash at the knights' leader Farnese.
    S1:E3 "Night of Miracles" The spirits pass Serpico and chase Guts to an abandoned mansion as he tells Farnese, who is unable to see them or Puck, that they are her god's "miracles". Guts quickly gets Farnese and the horse inside as the spirits possess hounds and use them to break in.
    S1:E4 "Revelations" After having a vision of Casca being burned at the stake, Guts returns to Godot's hut to check in on her. After he arrives, he receives the terrible news of Casca's disappearance. Guts struggles with his past demons along with the growing evil within his soul as he fears Casca is the only remainder of his sanity left.
    S1:E5 "Tower of Conviction" Guts encounters and kills a party of Kushan scouts, saving the life of Isidro who follows him out of admiration. Meanwhile, catching some starving refugees attacking a food wagon, Mozgus brings a refugee and her baby to the abbey; although her baby is cared for, Mozgus tortures her for her blasphemy.
    S1:E6 "Night Banquet of Burning at the Stake" As Casca is saved by the monstrous fetus, Guts encounters the mysterious Skull Knight for the first time in years since the Eclipse. Guts is disturbed to learn that the God Hand are using Albion as the site of a horrific ritual to restore Griffith's corporeal form, refusing to accept it as fate as he resumes his run towards the city with Isidro still following.
    S1:E7 "The Black Witch" Returning to camp while fearing persecution, Luca finds Pepe arrested by pagan hunters and tries to defend her before they are saved by Guts, who just arrived to Albion and dispatched the hunters while asking for Casca's whereabouts.
    S1:E8 "Reunion in the Den of Evil" Having arrived just in time, Guts saves Casca as the two are reunited. However the reunion is cut short once the Great Goat attacks Guts. Guts battles the fearsome Apostle as the Holy Iron Chain Knights retreat with Isidro, Puck and Nina escaping with Casca
    S1:E9 " Blood Flow of the Dead" After Nina named her as the witch he seeks, Mozgus places Casca in an iron maiden upon seeing her brand. But it causes the spirits of the tortured to manifest as a flesh-eating blob of blood with almost everyone able to escape. While Luca and Isidro use Jerome to free Nina, Guts forces Farnese to take him to the torture chamber where Puck tells him Casca is still alive.
    S1:E10 " Hell's Angels" Luca is spirited away by the mysterious observer, who reveals himself to her as a Beherit-like Apostle self-dubbed the Egg of the Perfect World. The Egg tells Luca his story and his long observation of everyone in Albion, detailing his ideals and the event to soon occur before fleeing from the Skull Knight.
    S1:E11 " Shadows of Idea" As Luca walks back to Albion after the Skull Knight took her to the outskirts, the Egg of the Perfect World swallows the dying Demon Child out of sympathy before resuming his climb up the Tower of Conviction. At the same time, enlisting Jerome and Nina's help, Isidro uses a makeshift pulley system to free Casca as she was about to be burned alive.
    S1:E12 "Those Who Cling, Those Who Struggle" Guts and his group fend off the flood of corpses with their fire while Luca saves Nina down below the tower. Meanwhile, the Egg of the Perfect World collects the energy of Albion's souls and sacrifices himself to incarnate Griffith in a corporeal form.
    S2:E1 " The Rent World" Guts and Puck take Casca back to Godot's home to find Godot has died. Griffith appears, wanting to know how meeting Guts would feel, and is pleased that he feels nothing and can continue to pursue his dream. Guts attempts to kill him but is stopped by Zodd who he duels fiercely until Zodd's sword shatters against the Dragon Slayer.
    S2:E2 " Winter Journey" A young witch, Schierke, watches Griffith from a distance. While tracking Guts, Serpico remembers how Farnese took him in as her servant, though she abused him terribly. Serpico learned he was the illegitimate son of Farnese's father and was therefore her half-brother, eventually becoming her bodyguard.
    S2:E3 "Banner of the Flying Sword" A Kushan occupied city is liberated by Griffith and his new Band of the Falcon: Composed of Nosferatu Zodd, Sir Locus the Moonlight Knight; the exiled Bakiraka assassin Rakshas, Grunbeld the Giant and Irvine the Archer. Joined by the clairvoyant girl Sonia, the Falcons later dealt with a Kushan legion that overwhelmed Midlanders under the command of Mule Wolflame.
    S2:E4 "Forest of Demonic Beasts" Farnese becomes depressed after Casca disappears and she gets lost in the forest trying to find her. A shepherd warns Guts' group about an imminent battle between Griffith's army and the Kushans and also about trolls infesting a nearby area.
    S2:E5 "The Astral World" Revealing an old friend of hers mentioned Guts and Casca would come to her abode, Flora learns of the plight befalling Enoch Village. But due to her failing health, Flora offers Morgan the aid of her apprentice Schierke to deal with the trolls.
    S2:E6 "Struggle Against the Demon Horde" Guts group arrive at Enoch Village but are turned away by the priest who claims that Schierke is merely a child in a costume, but allows them to stay when Guts claims Casca is a religious pilgrim. Isidro trains by himself and talks with Morgan about his past and Isidro's dreams for the future.
    S2:E7 " The Arcana of Invocation" With most of the trolls dead the village comes under attack from a giant Ogre. Guts fights the ogre but is attacked by a Kelpie from the nearby river. Serpico fights alongside Guts, pitting his wind magic against the Kelpie's water magic.
    S2:E8 "The Corruption of Qliphoth" Description Schierke realizes they have stumbled into a dangerous part of the Astral Realm known as Qliphoth. She telepathically communicates with Farnese and Casca in the trolls cave where the trolls are raping human women, forcing them to become pregnant and then devouring them after they give birth to troll offspring.
    S2:E9 "The Berserker Armor" Flora reveals a suit of Berserker Armor as guarded over the years. Griffith's demon soldiers break through her barrier, so the Skull Knight rides out to meet them. Guts suddenly collapses as Slan had damaged his soul, but he manages to keep walking and Schierke is astonished that Guts survived against an Apostle.
    S2:E10 "A Journey Begins in Flames" With the armor removing his limitations Guts unleashes all his strength, destroying Grunbeld’s shield and hammer and wounding him. Schierke explains that as Guts is fighting without limits he is tearing his own body apart, and while the armor removes his pain he will eventually bleed to death.
    S2:E11 "Proclaimed Omens" Guts party resume their journey to Puck’s home, an island called Skellig, half in the normal world and half in the astral plane, a home to both elves and mages. Farnese asks Schierke to teach her to use magic.
    S2:E12 "City of Humans" Guts and his friends approach the city of Vritannis where Isidro upsets Schierke about her witch’s clothing. Schierke learns that Vritannis traded in Kushan slaves, but all the slaves have been hanged as religious enemies of the Holy See.
