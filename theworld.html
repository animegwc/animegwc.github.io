Iroduku: The World in Colors

13 Episodes

Despite the kaleidoscopic magic ingrained in everyday life,
Hitomi Tsukishiro's monochrome world is deprived of emotion and feeling. On
a night as black and white as any other, amidst the fireworks spreading across
the sky, Hitomi's grandmother Kohaku conjures a spell, for which she has been
harnessing the moon's light for 60 years, to send Hitomi back in time to the
year 2018 when Kohaku was in high school.





Season & Episode Episode Name Description
S1:E1 "Where You Belong" Hitomi Tsukishiro, a girl descended from mages, endures a tragedy and loses the ability to see color as she grows up; Hitomi's grandmother, Kohaku, makes a request of her that takes Hitomi on a journey to the past.
S1:E2 "I Can't Stand Magic" Hitomi realizes she can see Yuito's art in color; Kohaku's parents talk Hitomi into enrolling in her grandmother's school, where she displays her magic powers to her new classmates.
S1:E3 "No Rain, No Rainbow" The club introductions for new students begin; the memory of Yuito's drawing leads Hitomi to visit the Photography Arts Club but she doesn't tell anyone she can't see color.
S1:E4 "Stop Calling Me Granny!" Kohaku returns from abroad and her behavior makes Hitomi nervous; the Photography Arts Club decides what to do for the cultural festival and goes to school at night to take pictures.
S1:E5 "A Modest Recipe" The Photography Arts Club rebrands and welcomes Kohaku to its ranks; Hitomi takes care of the magic shop and practices some magic.
S1:E6 "Golden Fish" Hitomi's star-sand gives Yuito a vision that also appears to Hitomi during a club photo shoot.
S1:E7 "The Burden of Venus" Summer break begins as the club plans an annual training camp; Kurumi's sister, Ichika, tries to help her work past a bad score on her mock exam.
S1:E8 "Fragile Fragments" Kohaku, Hitomi and Yuito look for a way to bring color back to Hitomi's vision.
S1:E9 "Wandering Words" Hitomi and Sho take day off to see sights that allow Hitomi to practice her photography.
S1:E10 "Monochromatic Crayon" Hitomi and Asagi have a heart-to-heart talk; Kohaku devises a way the Magic Photography Arts Club can contribute to the culture festival.
S1:E11 "The Waning Moon" As the club continues preparing materials for the cultural festival, Hitomi mysteriously vanishes while inside the darkroom.
S1:E12 "On This Bright, Shining Day" The culture festival opens and the club puts on its Magical Art Illusion, Asagi shows off her photographs and Yuito creates art for attendees; with Hitomi's time almost up, she and Yuito take a look around the school grounds.
S1:E13 "The World in Colors" As the culture festival draws to a close, Hitomi says goodbye to her friends before making the journey back home, but a problem with the star-sand watch sends Hitomi and Aoi somewhere unexpected.
